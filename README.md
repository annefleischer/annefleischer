## Experimenting with Vibe Coding

GitHub isn't where I usually live — but it's increasingly where the future of enablement is being built. Lately I've been exploring **vibe coding**: working alongside AI coding assistants like Claude, ChatGPT, and Cursor to prototype small tools, automations, and learning experiments by describing what I want and iterating in natural language until it works.

A few reasons I think this matters for someone in my seat:

- The teams I support are shipping AI-native products. Understanding how builders actually *work* with these tools makes me a sharper partner to product, sales, and engineering leadership.
- A lot of enablement logic — completion rules, certification scoring, content workflows, dashboard glue — currently lives in spreadsheets and tickets that quietly want to be small apps. Vibe coding makes that gap a lot smaller.
- The fluency I want my sellers and my enablement team to have with AI-driven product is the same fluency I'm building with code.

It's hands-on learning, not a career pivot. But I've been genuinely surprised by how far natural language plus a good model can take a non-engineer — and by how much it's reshaping what "technical" even means.
